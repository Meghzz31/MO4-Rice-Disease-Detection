Abstract

Rice is a staple food for millions worldwide, yet its productivity and quality are often threatened by diseases. This issue is particularly significant in Dakshina Kannada, Karnataka, known for its extensive cultivation of the MO4 rice variety, which is highly susceptible to diseases such as bacterial leaf blight, sheath blight, and neck blast. Early and accurate detection of these diseases is essential for effective management strategies. To address this, we propose a deep learning- based model leveraging convolutional neural networks (CNNs) for the reliable identification and classification of rice leaf diseases. Our research involved compiling a comprehensive dataset of healthy and diseased MO4 rice leaf images, meticulously annotated for precision. By fine-tuning state-of-the-art CNNs, the model demonstrates high accuracy, precision, recall, and F1 score in detecting diseases. Extensive testing ensures its robustness in real-world scenarios. The model offers farmers and agricultural officers a practical tool for early diagnosis, enabling timely intervention to reduce crop losses and promote sustainable agriculture. Experimental results highlight its potential to revolutionize rice disease management in Dakshina Kannada and similar regions, paving the way for intelligent agricultural systems that enhance productivity and ensure food security..
